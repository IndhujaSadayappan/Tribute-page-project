Mother Teresa



Mother Teresa devoted her life to serving the destitute and the dying in Calcutta and around the world. Her compassion and selfless service earned her the Nobel Peace Prize and the admiration of people from all walks of life.
Link to her Biography

1. Founding the Missionaries of Charity (1950): Established this organization in Calcutta, India, to care for the "hungry, naked, homeless, sick, crippled, blind, and lepers."

2. Nobel Peace Prize (1979): Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for her humanitarian work and dedication to helping the poor and sick.

3. Over 5,000 Members Worldwide: The Missionaries of Charity grew to include more than 5,000 members, operating in over 130 countries.

4. Homes for the Dying: Created homes such as Nirmal Hriday in Calcutta to provide dignity and care to the dying, regardless of their faith or background.

5. Orphanages and Schools: Established orphanages, schools, and homes for abandoned children, offering education and care to those in need.

6. Hospices and Leprosy Clinics: Developed hospices for the terminally ill and clinics for people suffering from leprosy, improving their quality of life and offering medical care.

7. Global Influence: Her work inspired the formation of similar charitable organizations and initiatives worldwide, influencing global attitudes towards humanitarian aid.

8. Author of Books: Authored several books, including "No Greater Love" and "A Call to Mercy," sharing her experiences and teachings.

9. Beatification (2003): Beatified by the Roman Catholic Church, a step towards canonization, recognizing her as "Blessed Teresa of Calcutta."

10. Canonization as a Saint (2016): Canonized as a saint by Pope Francis, officially recognized as Saint Teresa of Calcutta.

11. International Awards and Honors: Received numerous awards and honors from various organizations and governments for her humanitarian work.

12. Advocate for the Unborn: Voiced strong opposition to abortion and advocated for the protection of unborn children and the sanctity of life.

13. Support for the Poor: Focused on the care and support of the destitute, the sick, and the marginalized, bringing global attention to their plight.

14. Simple Living: Lived a life of simplicity and poverty, setting an example by working directly among the poor and leading by example.

15. Legacy of Compassion: Left a lasting legacy of compassion and service, with her organization continuing to operate and serve those in need around the world.
